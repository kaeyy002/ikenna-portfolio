Case Study 01

Unsaid

01

Unsaid is an anonymous messaging app built around the feelings people hold back. Not to encourage recklessness, but to create space where honesty costs less. It sits in the emotional territory between vulnerability and relief.

App Design Brand Strategy Content System Front-end Dev

View Live Site ↗
Deep Purple
Void
Electric
Breath

Purple for depth and mystery. Lime for the confession that breaks through.

Core Audience
Gen Z and young millennials (18-28) carrying the weight of things they haven't said to people in their lives. They process emotions online. They want to be honest without the consequences of being known.
Unsaid · Strategy

The Brand Approach

Brand Voice
Quiet but certain. The brand never shouts. Copy is intimate, second-person, present tense. It reads like a message written specifically for you. Every line should feel like it was pulled from someone's drafts folder.
Content Pillars
50% Emotional Resonance — content people recognise themselves in immediately.

30% Community — anonymised user confessions, shared moments, real responses.

20% Product — features framed as emotional tools, never as tech specs.
Growth Loop
Discovery through relatable content → Download to see what others have said to them → Send something to someone → That person downloads to see it → Cycle repeats. The product does the marketing.
Platform Strategy
TikTok: Confession POV videos. The "things I never said" format. Emotional hooks in the first two seconds.

Instagram: Quote cards, story polls, shareable confessions.

X: Short, specific, relatable one-liners and threads.
My Role
Built the product independently, end to end. Product ideation, UX/UI design, front-end development, brand naming, copywriting, content strategy, and the overall visual identity. Unsaid is entirely mine.
The Core Insight
People don't want to say the hard thing. They want something that makes it safe enough to say. Unsaid's job is to reduce the emotional cost of honesty. That's the product, and that's the brand.
Case Study 02

LodgeKonnect

02

LodgeKonnect was developed as my final-year Information Technology project. Rather than building a purely academic application, I chose to solve a real problem affecting university students by creating a platform that simplifies the search for off-campus accommodation and compatible roommates.

Product Design Front-end Dev UX Research Brand Strategy

View Live Site ↗

Grounded, approachable, and trustworthy. Colours that feel safe to students under stress.

Target Audience
University students at FUTO dealing with the annual stress of finding safe, affordable accommodation near campus. Specifically those who want to share costs with a compatible roommate but have no trusted way to find one.
The Problem

Every academic session, thousands of students struggle to find safe, affordable accommodation near campus. Most rely on WhatsApp groups, Facebook posts, or physically walking around the school environment hoping to stumble across something suitable. The process is exhausting, time-consuming, and often exposes students to scams and poor housing decisions.

Another challenge I observed was finding compatible roommates. Many students wanted to share accommodation to cut costs, but there was no trusted platform to connect people with similar budgets, schedules, and lifestyles.

I'd experienced both problems during my own time at university. So I built something to fix them.

What LodgeKonnect Does
  • Browse available lodges with photos and detailed information
  • Compare accommodation options before travelling to view
  • Connect with potential roommates based on compatibility
  • View location, pricing, and amenities in one place
  • Reduce the uncertainty that usually comes with accommodation searches
My Role

I led the entire project independently, from initial concept through to a deployed, functioning platform.

Product Ideation User Research Brand Naming UX/UI Design Front-end Dev Copywriting Content Strategy User Journey Planning
Why It Matters

LodgeKonnect is more than a final-year submission. It's a solution to something I personally experienced and watched peers struggle with. Building it taught me how to combine technology, branding, and user-centred design into a product with real social value. Unlike general property listing sites, LodgeKonnect was built specifically for university students and the unique pressures they face at the start of each academic year.

Proposal

How I Would Contribute at PixelHauze

Note — This is a proposal, not completed work What follows is how I would approach growing PixelHauze's social presence and content strategy if I joined the team. These are recommendations based on my understanding of the creative agency space, not results I have previously achieved for them.
Brand Audit Observations
Creative studios at PixelHauze's stage often produce exceptional client work but struggle to communicate that quality on social media. The work exists but the narrative around it doesn't. The opportunity isn't making the work better — it's making the work visible in a way that builds trust with the right clients before they've even made contact.
The Core Opportunity
PixelHauze's target clients, startup founders and SME owners, are making decisions based on perceived credibility. They don't always know what great design looks like, but they can feel when a studio understands their world. Social content that educates and demonstrates in equal measure is what bridges that gap between great work and qualified enquiries.

PixelHauze · Proposed Rollout

90-Day Strategy

Days 1 – 30
Build the Foundation
  • Audit and rebuild all social profiles: bios, links, visual consistency
  • Create a 30-day content calendar with pillar rotation built in
  • Launch a "Design 101 for Founders" education series
  • Post daily on Instagram, three times a week on LinkedIn
  • Establish a brand voice guide and reusable visual templates
  • Engage meaningfully with 20 target accounts each day
Days 31 – 60
Build the Audience
  • Launch the first client case study series as a 3-part deep dive
  • Start TikTok with before/after brand transformation videos
  • Introduce a "Design Audit" offer: low ticket, high perceived value
  • Collaborate with two complementary creators in adjacent spaces
  • Begin building an email list via a free brand checklist lead magnet
  • Weekly "Pixel Picks" content: design inspiration with real commentary
Days 61 – 90
Build the Pipeline
  • Launch "PixelHauze Studio Sessions": live Q&A on Instagram and LinkedIn
  • Amplify two or three top-performing organic posts with paid spend
  • Create a case study PDF lead magnet with an email nurture sequence
  • Pitch podcast appearances in design, entrepreneurship, and startup spaces
  • Add discovery call CTAs consistently across all touchpoints
  • Establish a monthly reporting rhythm: reach, engagement, inbound enquiries
